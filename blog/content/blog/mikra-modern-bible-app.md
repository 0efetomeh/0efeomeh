# Mikra: a modern Bible app for thoughtful, offline-first study

## A quieter reading experience

Mikra started from a simple idea: Scripture study should feel focused, calm, and immediate. The interface avoids clutter so the text stays at the center of the experience.

## Design principles

- Content-first layout with generous spacing and restrained visual noise.
- Offline-first behavior so study remains reliable when connectivity drops.
- Careful typography and subtle hierarchy so reading does not feel fatigued.
- Progressive enhancement so useful modern features never get in the way of the text.

## What we learned

The strongest improvements came from subtraction: fewer controls, simpler navigation, and a tighter relationship between interface and content. In a reading tool, restraint is a feature.
